Why This Matters
The Problem I've Seen

Passionate teams,
fragile foundations.

Early in my journey I witnessed something that stayed with me — a brilliant team doing genuinely meaningful work, worn down by systems that couldn't hold the weight of their ambitions. They had purpose in abundance. What they lacked was structure.

That tension — between the urgency of mission and the unglamorous work of building durable organisations — is where I have spent my career. Not as an outside observer. As someone who has sat in those rooms, walked those fields, and stayed long enough to see what actually holds.

"Purpose is powerful. But it needs structure to survive — and strategy to scale."

— Radhika Kothari, Founder, The Ark Impact
01

Mission-drift is silent and common

Organisations lose their thread not through bad intentions, but through growth without clarity — new roles, new funders, new pressures. I help you find the thread again.

02

Strategy without groundedness fails

Too much consultancy advice is written for contexts it doesn't understand. My approach is built from the inside out — grounded in field realities, not just frameworks.

03

Good work deserves to last

Grassroots change, conservation, social enterprise — this work is too important to be undermined by weak systems. I'm here to make sure it doesn't have to be.

04

I'm not a fly-in consultant

I work in small cohorts, deeply, over time. You get me — not a junior team, not a templated output. Bespoke, honest, and built for your reality.

Three Pathways

From vision
to real momentum

Each engagement is shaped around you — your organisation, your moment, your people. I don't offer off-the-shelf programmes. What I offer is genuine attentiveness and the experience to know what will actually hold. These three pathways are starting points, not boxes.

01

Align

When direction feels uncertain or priorities have drifted. We work together to surface what matters, clarify your positioning, and set leadership teams up with a coherent path forward.
